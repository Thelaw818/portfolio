Color Theory Overview

Color theory is the study of how colors interact, evoke emotions, and can be combined effectively. Below is a glossary of key terms:

Primary Colors
Red, blue, and yellow; the base colors that cannot be made by mixing others.
Secondary Colors
Green, orange, and purple; created by mixing two primary colors.
Tertiary Colors
Colors made by mixing a primary and a secondary color.

Typography Overview

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and visually appealing. Here are some key terms:

Serif
A font with small decorative lines at the ends of characters.
Sans Serif
A font without decorative lines at the ends of characters.
Kerning
The adjustment of space between two characters.

Font Types Article

Fonts can be categorized into various types. Below are some examples:

  • Serif: Classic and formal (e.g., Times New Roman).
  • Sans Serif: Modern and clean (e.g., Arial).
  • Script: Decorative and handwritten (e.g., Brush Script).
